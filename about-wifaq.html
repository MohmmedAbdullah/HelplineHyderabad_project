About Wifaq

Allah has given all the creatures in this world the understanding of their gain and loss but has created man entirely ignorant; he is not aware of his gain and loss; while a chick that begins to peck grain from birth leaves out pebbles considering them harmful whereas even if an ember is given to an infant, not only it grasps it but also does not refrain from putting it into the mouth too.

Hence it is imperative for every person to acquire knowledge to understand gain and loss and lead life accordingly and since the Creator of human better knows that which is useful or harmful to him, it is obligatory for every Muslim male and female to acquire minimal knowledge necessary to differentiate legitimate and illegitimate and lawful and unlawful and the system to facilitate the fulfilment of this obligation in every segment of the community, in every section, locality, mosque and village is named Maktab; Alhamdulillah, this system has been present from Prophetic Era and is persistent till today in different forms and in different places in its diffusive manner and Insha Allah will continue till the Day of Resurrection.

In this age, inspite of the entire relevance, efficacy and presence of religious schools in every locality the trend of the commoners and elite is more towards English schools due to which a large section of Muslims is moving away from religious education and religious ignorance is increasing day after day. Therefore, in order to overcome these circumstances, while various efforts are being made, one among them is the creation and stability of these religious schools and Alhamdulillah, Idara Wifaq-ul-Makatib took this initiative three years ago, without relying on any apparent source, purely with confidence in Allah and Alhamdulillah being successful to a great extent in its aims and objectives has been marching towards growth.

The basic aims and objectives of Idara Wafaq-ul-Makatib include the fact that while Alhamdulillah religious schools are functional in various areas, an educational system for basic religious education ought to be present for students receiving formal education during daytime (whose numbers are multiple times of madrasa students) that has discipline and quality. Hence with this objective and with the assistance of few colleagues, this work began with four Maktabs under the supervision of Hazrat Moulana Shah Jamal-ur-Rahman Sahab (DB) and Alhamdulillah today __ Maktabs, ___ teachers, __ workers, ___ students are utilising this system. To make it more effective and to strengthen and spread it, a few issues have been basically determined in consultation with Hazrat Shah Sahab and Alhamdulillah those associated with the organisation and general public are witnessing the benefits of these issues; they are: (1) Establishment of collective and unified platform (2) Orientation and regulation of each Maktab (3) Ensuring quality education

1) Collective and Unified Platform:

Collectivity is the fundamental aspect for the strength and stability of any work; a person might be intelligent, clever and brave to any extent, but he cannot accomplish the feats that could be achieved easily even by a small group; hence as long as these Maktabs were functioning in individual capacity no significant benefits were apparent and despite ensuring sound management and better education three were clouds of despair.

Hence people associated individually with Maktabs in different locations were anxious and after collective consultations determined that unless taking an elderly person a patron are submitted to someone, stability in work shall remain a distant dream; with consensus it was determined to obtain the supervision of Hazrat Shah Jamal-ur-Rahman Sahab and it is the privilege of the organisation that Hazrat accepted; as a result a unified platform connected directly to Hazrat and all the members of Makatib associated, came into existence in September 2013 under the name Wifaq-ul-Makatib.

Activities of the office

(1) Create awareness in public for establishment of religious schools

(2) Provide right guidance to people concerned about establishment of Makatib

(3) Make efforts for the establishment of new organised Maktabs each month

(4) Take steps to develop educational standards in existing and established schools

(5) Provide essential goods required in organised schools

(6) Conduct male and female workshop (training classes) for teachers teaching in schools.

1) To raise awareness in public for establishment of Maktabs, heads and in-charge persons of various mosques in different circles are associated and introductory programmes are conducted and it is ensured that the in-charge persons of each mosque reflect for the establishment of organised schools in their mosques; Alhamdulillah, in this way many Maktabs have been established in various mosques.

2) Right guidance to people concerned for the establishment of schools: Those people who are prepared to work in their mosque and area are called to the office or a representative is sent there and complete guidance is provided from start to end and their names, phone number etc are registered at the office and constant communication is initiated with them due to which mutual cooperation and support is facilitated.

3) The in-charge persons of unorganised schools are contacted through central coordinator and steps are taken to organise these schools and the establishment of new organised schools each month is ascertained.

4) A system is made to raise educational standards in existing schools through assessment tests for which examiners named local coordinators are determined who test each student of every school and report the educational status of each student to the school administrators and if due to lack of efforts and educational weakness, lessons need to be repeated then the examiner shall have the option to repeat.

5) The goods that are needed in organised schools are made available at cost price so that organised schools could start expediently in each area and locality and the system for children’s religious education and training could be brought under control.

6) The top secret for the success of any educational institution is that the teachers and administrators of that institution are trained, psychological experts and are qualified; therefore, at Wifaq-ul-Makatib, it is ensured the teachers who teach in schools have been trained and hence for this purpose classes are conducted periodically through the office in which complete training along with food is provided. Also efforts are made to create awareness through monthly and quarterly consultations.

The System of Maktab:

1) For the strength of any educational institution, two departments need to be basically strong (1) Administration (2) Education; since focussing on administration without paying attention to education is infact deceiving people in the name of education and focussing on education alone without administration is also a futile exercise and hence both these departments are crucial. Thus, in order to strengthen the administration a mechanism is devised that wherever a school is being initiated, its organised establishment is announced with complete vigour; for this purpose, primarily, communicating the importance, utility and efficacy of a Maktab and ill-effects of distancing from it to every person belonging to each and every home of the locality through two to three Friday prayers or programme or meeting or social gathering, SMS, WhatsApp, pamphlets etc., are encouraged to get their children enrolled to the Maktab before the specified date and at the time of admission all the conditions, rules and regulations are notified to the guardians as well as students so that parents and children are bound by them.

2) At the time of admission, uniform, ID card, bag, books, report cards, and fee cards are handed over so that every student of the Maktab comes to school with complete discipline and uniformity.

3) In the Maktab, if initially two teachers are available then students are enrolled proportionately so that quality of education is not affected; remaining students are kept on waiting list and after arrangement of additional teachers they are enrolled and by increasing shifts it is ensured that maximum benefit is derived from minimum teachers and due to increase in shifts, the mosque reverberates with the remembrance of Allah and Prophet apart from prayer times and it facilitates the students to attend Maktab along with their tuitions.

4) Although the Maktab is of just one period, uniform creates manners and etiquettes and importance and fondness for Islamic dress in students.

5) Group education is arranged with the help of blackboard so that not a single moment of any student is lost.

6) Out of compulsion, if any student wants to take leave for a day or two from school, the guardians are bound to approach the office before hand and give request for leave; the leave shall be valid at the approval of request; violation shall lead to suitable action in consultation with guardians.

7) Students who are punctual and bright in studies are awarded periodically and particularly during quarterly and annual functions through punctual cards and suitable prizes so that it may develop interest in others students.

8) In order that each school is self-reliant without being dependent on anyone there is a fee or sponsorship structure; all the students either pay their own fees or study with sponsorship from virtue-seekers, due to which teachers’ salaries and other administrative expenses are taken care of to a large extent.

9) It is essential for the parents to be concerned about the education and training of their children and hence efforts are made to contemplate parents through parent-teacher meeting.

10) The punctuality of students depends on the punctuality of teachers and hence attendance register is maintained for teachers so that students also become accustomed to punctuality.

11) At the end of the year annual function of each Maktab is held in which students take active part and prizes are awarded to meritorious students.

These all were the administrative activities of Maktab

Educational Activities of Maktab

1) As soon as the students come to the Maktab, before beginning education, all the students are made to stand and recite Hamd and Naat so that work begins with glorification of Allah and praise of the Prophet and temperament of children inclines towards humming of Naats and poems in this musical age.

2) Later, the students sit on their seats and first and foremost the teacher writes the lesson on the board and then the teacher collectively reads the lesson to the students and ask the students to read; the biggest advantage of this is that the students would learn the lesson in the Maktab itself and they are totally engaged from start to end.

3) In the event of two or more teachers, one of them is made the head of Maktab.

4) All possible attempts and efforts are made to ensure superior quality of education of the Maktab; an examiner is determined for six to eight schools who conducts examination for the students of all schools and communicate the performance to the administrators so that they could remove educational weakness in the students through teachers before the next review (next month).

5) Normally the parents are encouraged to attend the Maktab and receive monthly progress report so that they remain concerned for the religious education and training of students.

6) Once in two to three months a group meeting of the guardians is held in which students’ training at home is specifically encouraged and attention is drawn to social issues that need reformation.

7) In addition to educational monitoring, steps are taken for psychological and social traning of students through Namaz chart or weekly or biweekly remedial session.
